Sensei

The Problem

How can we empower Montessori teachers with the information they need?

The Solution

Use novel sensing technologies to deliver interaction data directly to the teacher.

Sensei: Sensing Educational Interaction is a distributed sensor network system designed to study interaction between students, teachers, and materials in the Montessori classroom. Proximity sensors are placed throughout the classroom to measure social interactions and learning engagement. The teachers use insights from this information to make the best decisions to help their students learn. To learn more or to contact the team, please visit our website.

I was involved in deploying the Sensei system in 3 Montessori schools, interviewing teachers to design a streamlined deployment method and determine use cases, and developed a video camera system with Raspberry Pi to automatically transfer video footage to lab computers.
