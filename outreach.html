Education Outreach

The Problem

How can we help students learn how to learn?

The Solution

Provide opportunities for students to have more learning agency.

My involvement in education outreach ranges across many grades and topics. I am dedicated to creating better educational opportunities for students to grow their strengths. I aim to create opportunities for students to challenge themselves and develop key habits of mind such as creativity, curiosity, and confidence.

My Involvements

VP of Outreach for the Society of Women Engineers SWE is dedicated to inspiring young girls to be inspired by STEM. Serving over 3000 girls from all over the globe every year, MIT SWE strives to help repair the leaky STEM pipeline and encourage more girls to stay in STEM. Throughout my time in SWE, I started seven new outreach programs. From connecting collegebound high school girls worldwide to MIT mentors to empowering underprivileged middle school students with design thinking, my programs targeted students from all backgrounds to be inspired to learn.

Four Weeks for America I assisted a Teach for America teacher in an 11th grade math class at IDEA Donna School. There, I developed new assessments for understanding student progress and taught multiple lessons on topics ranging from design thinking to robotic controls.

CETI: China Educational Teaching Initiative I was the lead teacher for three science & engineering camps across Taiwan and Hong Kong for high school and college students. I led eight MIT students in developing 6 weeks of curriculum for the STEM camps.

Citizen Schools This was my foray into teching design thinking to a much younger crowd: sixth graders. I designed a 10 week design thinking curriculum complete with lessons, daily design activities, and final design projects. At the end, students in the class said they learned about working on a team, creating their own ideas, and compromising. Really goes to show the power of design thinking, even for young students!

Design for America Explored multiple ways to spread a design thinking mindset to very technically minded students. I led multiple classes on design thinking, coordinated an Arduino hackshop with 75 attendees to teach Arduino in the context of healthcare, and designed a leadership training program for new leaders on the Design for America executive team.

JV Inventeams I designed the program curriculum used by 20 schools across the nation. The activities are created to promote innovative thinking and develop new skills. Projects I created include designing hydroponic gardening systems, building hydraulic drawbridges, and creating new purpose-led shoe designs. Find more details here.

Singapore Leadership Programme Led a group of college freshmen at the Singapore University of Technology & Design (SUTD) in starting social service organizations at the university. Led a section on facing fears and jumpstarted campus-wide programs in campus wellness, community volunteering, and international development.

Academic Teaching Initiative Reformed the SAT writing curriculum and taught a class of over 100 students.

Private Tutor Tutored for over 6 years in subjects ranging from biology to writing to calculus.

Science Olympiad Coach Mentored various Science Olympiad topics including Mousetrap Vehicle, Disease Detectives, Experimental Design, Chemistry Lab, and Forensics. Each subject involved a class of 15 students.

Speech & Debate Coach Coached multiple students in Lincoln Douglas Debate who later attended the Tournament of Champions, the most prestigious US debate tournament.
