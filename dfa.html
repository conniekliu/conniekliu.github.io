MIT Design For America

The Problem

How can we empower college students with the skills, confidence, and creativity to make an impact?

The Solution

Build a community of passionate changemakers to help each other build impactful projects to solve pressing problems.

MIT Design for America is a club dedicated to making a social impact in the community. We connect students with real people to solve real problems.

As DFA, we run hackshops and project teams throughout the year. Project teams work on yearlong projects tackling problems in our local community such as homelessness and poor education. Hackshops are monthly skill-building workshops open to all students and built to help them acquire new skills and learn about how to apply those to human-centered design.

DFA was born out of the idea that making a difference doesn't have to wait until after graduation. Today, DFA is the hub of social impact through design & engineering at MIT. We strive to create a community of passionate changemakers who support each other in making a difference.

My Role

I founded DFA in 2013 and served as president, project team director, and new initiatives director for the past few years. I led the publicity efforts to attract over 450 students to DFA and grow our influence across campus. I also initiated the core components of DFA, including hackshops, project teams, skill chops, and design critiques. For the project teams, I wrote and managed the development of a human centered design guide to help teach newcomers. Some of the design guide pages are featured above.

But what I love most is fostering new leaders. Design for America is about more than making a difference: it's about helping students develop skills that can't be learned in the classroom. DFA students tackle unsolved problems and in the process, develop the empathy, creativity, and passion that they carry forward with them. I want DFA members to have the creative confidence to pursue their ideas and not be afraid to be passionate about impact making. I spend a lot of time thinking about how we can improve DFA to better foster leadership development and build a strong community of passionate people helping each other while helping the world. DFA is my personal design challenge to help encourage the future changemakers of America.
