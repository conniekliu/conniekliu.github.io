    • LINE FOLLOWING ROBOT

    An autonomous robot with attitude

    Our final project for 2.678, MIT's Electronics for Mechanical Systems class, was to build a line following robot that autonomously navigates a maze. We were given an Arduino, an infrared sensor array, and a robot frame to start. The maze consisted of a 1 inch wide black line extending through the course. However, there were also obstacles such as Figure 8 loops, garbled lines, blank spots, and more. Success was judged by speed, accuracy, and ability to get through the whole track.

    My partner and I were able to complete the source in 1:37, ranking in the top scoring robots. However, in future iterations of the robot, I would have included a more efficient search algorithm that utilized path prediction rather than the inefficient 180 scan used for this version.

    Lessons Learned

    Arduino Programming: In programming the Arduino, I utilized delay functions, interrupt pins, and serial commands. We also tuned thresholds for the sensor values to control for any noise from the sensors and to make the software more robust. Depending on this sensor input, the robot would either continue straight, turn left, turn right, or scan for black to return to the path.     Electronics Testing: In order to debug our circuit, we used a multimeter to measure current, voltage, and continuity across different leads. In addition, as a prerequisite to the autonomous robot challenge, we designed test circuits to send smoothed signals using an external power supply and a waveform generator.
